## Why Tapering Works

In the 2-3 weeks before a race, reducing training volume allows glycogen stores to top up, micro-damage in muscle fibers to repair, neuromuscular fatigue to dissipate, and motivation to rebuild. Research consistently shows a 40-60% volume reduction in the final week before a race improves performance by 2-3% vs no taper.

The biggest taper mistake is reducing intensity alongside volume. Keep intensity in the taper — remove volume only. One quality session per week during the taper maintains neuromuscular sharpness.

## Marathon Taper Protocol (3 Weeks)

Week 3 before race (last big week): Complete the last long run (20-22km at easy pace). Total weekly volume ~90% of peak training week.

Week 2 before race: Reduce total volume to 65-70% of peak. Include one race-pace workout of 8-10km (e.g., 2km easy + 6km at goal marathon pace + 2km easy). All other runs easy.

Week 1 before race: Reduce to 40-50% of peak volume. Tuesday: 5-6km with 3km at goal pace. Wednesday: easy 30 min. Thursday: rest or easy 20 min jog. Friday: rest or 15 min easy. Saturday: 10-15 min easy shakeout. Sunday: race.

Do not add extra miles in taper week because you "feel fresh." Trust the process.

## Half Marathon Taper Protocol (2 Weeks)

Week 2 before race: Reduce to 70-75% of peak volume. Include one quality session of 5-6km at race pace or slightly faster.

Week 1 before race: Reduce to 40-50% of peak volume. Tuesday: 4-5km with 2-3km at goal half marathon pace. Thursday: easy 20-25 min. Friday: rest or easy 20 min. Saturday: rest or 10 min easy shakeout. Sunday: race.

## 10K Taper Protocol (1 Week)

Reduce volume by 35-40% the week before. Monday or Tuesday: 5-6 x 400m at goal 10K pace with 90-second rest. Easy running the rest of the week. Rest or 10 min shakeout the day before.

## 5K Taper Protocol (4-5 Days)

Reduce volume by 25-30% the 4-5 days before. Two days before: 4-6 x 200m at goal 5K pace with full recovery. Day before: rest or 10 min jog. Race day: 10-15 min warm-up including 2-3 x 100m strides.

## Common Taper Mistakes

Taper madness: Feeling sluggish, heavy, or anxious during the taper is normal and does not indicate lost fitness. It is the body adapting. Trust the data — if REI was improving before the taper, fitness is there.

Adding miles: Resist the urge to add volume because runs feel easy. The freshness is the point.

New shoes or gear: Never race in shoes worn for the first time on race day. Taper week is not the time to experiment.

Nutrition: Do not dramatically change diet during taper. Slight carbohydrate increase in the final 2-3 days (carb loading) is appropriate for marathon and half marathon only.

## Metrics During Taper

Expect HR to be slightly elevated on easy taper runs compared to peak training — this is normal as the body isn't as deeply adapted to the lower load. AE may appear to worsen slightly during taper. This does not indicate fitness loss; it reflects the change in training stimulus. REI during taper is not a reliable fitness indicator — use pre-taper trends instead.
